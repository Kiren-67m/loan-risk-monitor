Background

Project Overview

This project began as a timed competition entry at AITCC 2025 (America's Innovate IT Collegiate Conference), where our team placed 2nd in the Analyze IT Challenge. The original task: predict loan defaults from messy, multi-table banking data under time pressure.

Post-competition, I extended the project into something more ambitious — an operational intelligence system that transforms static prediction outputs into continuous, actionable risk monitoring. The system now includes monthly KPI tracking, risk segmentation with intervention prioritization, interactive dashboards, and AI-generated executive risk reports powered by Claude API.

The core analytical insight driving the entire system: payment behavior features engineered from raw monthly records (on-time rate, cumulative delinquent days) are far more predictive of default than traditional credit metrics like credit score or income — with correlations above 0.70 versus near-zero for conventional indicators.

Executive Summary


Portfolio health in January 2023 is strong across primary metrics: 183,596 active loans show a 98.20% on-time payment rate with only 1.80% delinquency and 1.80% high-risk concentration. However, the Critical risk band represents a material concentration risk, comprising 15.0% of the portfolio (27,585 loans) with a 99.81% average default probability and significantly degraded on-time performance at 51.85%. This bifurcated profile—healthy mainstream portfolio offsetting a distressed tail—requires immediate intervention on the Critical segment before deterioration accelerates across Month 2.


Key Risk Signals


  • Critical band default concentration: 27,585 loans (15.0% of portfolio) carry 0.9981 average default probability with on-time rate of only 0.5185, representing ~$2.0 billion in principal at extreme risk ($74,195 average principal × 27,585 loans).

  • Top-10 loans at certainty of default: 10 individual loans each show 1.0000 default probability, with principals of $242,229 per loan and delinquency already present (range: 2–11 days past due), totaling ~$2.42M in immediate distress.

  • Home equity and credit line vulnerability: Of the top-10 intervention priority loans, 60% are home equity lines and 40% are credit lines, concentrated in coastal and cyclical-economy states (FL, MN, CT, RI, NJ), suggesting potential regional economic stress.

Recommended Actions


1. Activate immediate workout protocol for top-10 loans (L14492-543328 through L36747-848034): Assign dedicated collections case managers to each loan within 5 business days. Prioritize L35724-203317 (11 days delinquent, 0.3333 on-time rate) and L15625-229307 (7 days delinquent, GU jurisdiction) for accelerated contact and recovery negotiation; target resolution or restructuring within 30 days.


2. Establish Critical band triage framework: Segment the 27,585 Critical loans into sub-cohorts by on-time rate (≤0.33, 0.34–0.66, 0.67–1.0) and state; initiate targeted forbearance/modification outreach to the ≤0.33 cohort (estimated 9,000–10,000 loans) before Month 2 deterioration accelerates, with success target of 40% retention in portfolio.


3. Stress-test home equity and credit line exposure: Given 60% of top-10 risks are home equity lines, run scenario modeling on Florida and Connecticut portfolios (states with 2 loans each in critical priority list) assuming 3–5% additional monthly migration to Critical band; establish monthly monitoring thresholds to trigger proactive rate or term adjustments before Month 2 deterioration cycle.
